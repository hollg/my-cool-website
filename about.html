About the Department of Administrative Services

Our Mission

The Department of Administrative Services (DAS) exists to provide comprehensive administrative support to federal agencies, ensuring efficient government operations while maintaining the highest standards of public accountability and fiscal responsibility.

History

Founded in 1947 under Executive Order 9877, the Department of Administrative Services was established in the post-war era to consolidate and streamline administrative functions across the federal government. Over the past seven decades, DAS has evolved to meet the changing needs of modern government while maintaining its core commitment to efficiency and service excellence.

Organizational Structure

DAS is organized into five principal divisions:

  • Office of Procurement and Acquisition Services
  • Division of Facilities Management and Operations
  • Human Resources Coordination Bureau
  • Inter-Agency Liaison Office
  • Administrative Policy and Compliance Division

Leadership

The Department is led by the Director of Administrative Services, who reports directly to the Office of Management and Budget. The current director is appointed by the President and confirmed by the Senate.

Key Achievements

Since its inception, DAS has achieved numerous milestones in government efficiency, including the implementation of standardized procurement procedures (1952), the establishment of the Federal Administrative Database System (1989), and the successful digitization of inter-agency coordination processes (2015).
